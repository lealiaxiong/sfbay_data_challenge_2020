Analysis of discrepancies between measurements reported in different units

I plotted the empirical cumulative distribution function (ECDF) of all the data for each measurement to visualize any differences in the distribution of data reported in different units.

Left: ECDF of all data for the measurement, colored by reported unit. Right: Separate ECDFs for each reported unit. Shaded region is bootstrap 95% confidence interval (CI).

Mercury in fish

Click on the entries in the legend to show/hide.

The results for ug/g dw skew higher than for ug/g ww. However, most of the results overlap.

Mercury in sediment

Click on the entries in the legend to show/hide.

Note that ng/g is equivalent to ug/kg. Even through there are two different units (ug/kg dw and mg/kg dw) for mercury in sediment, the data appear to follow a reasonable distribution. Attempting to correct the units (multiplying values in mg/kg dw by 1000 to conver to ug/kg dw) results in a bimodal distribution. Thus, I continued the analysis without converting the units.
